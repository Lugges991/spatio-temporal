The SlowFast Architecture

SlowFast is a two stream neural network architecture inspired by the visual processing system of the mammalian brain. It consists of a "slow" and a "fast" pathway, which in turn are separate neural networks. The slow and fast pathway are connected via fuse connections in order to exchange information.
The slow pathway is characterized by low temporal resolution but high channel capacity. The fast pathway has high temporal resolution and lower channel capacity. The backbones for the two pathways are 3D ResNets101 with Non-Local Blocks.

In the lateral geniculate nucleus a first division of incoming optical information from the optical nerve into separate streams happens. Model Analysis:

The SlowFast model was trained using distributed synchronized stochasic gradient descent on a cluster of 128 GPUs. The trained model was evaluated on the Kinetics dataset. More info in the thesis.

Practical Analysis of the SlowFast Architecture:

The following figure evaluates the runtime costs of different architectures on current NVIDIA GPUs, lower is better. Slow Fast has the lowest runtime cost on all devices.

The next figure plots top-1 accuracy against runtime cost, higher is better. Again, SlowFast is clearly outperforming its competitors.
